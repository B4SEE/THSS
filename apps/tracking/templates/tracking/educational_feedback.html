What made this email suspicious?

  • Urgency "Expires in 24 hours" — attackers create artificial time pressure to stop you thinking carefully. Legitimate IT departments give advance notice through internal procedures.
  • Threat "You will lose access" — emotional threats are a hallmark of phishing. Real service expiry notices are routine, not emergencies.
  • Link Hovering over the button would show a URL that does not belong to your organisation or to Microsoft's real domains (accounts.microsoft.com, login.microsoftonline.com).
  • Sender The actual sender address is never from Microsoft's real domain. Always expand the sender name to see the full email address.
  • Process Password changes for Microsoft 365 go through your organisation's IT portal or Windows settings — never via an emailed link.
